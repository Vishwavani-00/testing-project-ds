7Business Insights
📈 Trend: Sales show a mild upward trend over 2022–2023, with an average daily growth of approximately 1.5%, indicating healthy category expansion across all 3 stores.
📅 Weekly Pattern: Saturday and Sunday consistently drive 15–25% higher sales than weekdays. Promotions on weekdays can help smooth out demand.
🗓️ Monthly Seasonality: December is the highest-performing month (holiday season), followed by July–August. February and March show the weakest demand.
🤖 ML Advantage: Linear Regression outperformed all other approaches by learning from lag features and rolling statistics — confirming that recent sales history is the strongest predictor of near-term demand.
🔗 Lag Correlation: sales_lag_1 and rolling_mean_7 show the highest correlation with current sales (r > 0.85), meaning very recent momentum is the primary demand driver.
📊 Forecast Outlook: The 30-day forecast shows a -10.5% change vs the prior 30-day average, with daily sales expected between 2,105 and 3,202 units.
8Recommendations
  1. Deploy Linear Regression for weekly demand planning — retrain weekly with a rolling 90-day window to keep predictions fresh.
  2. Increase stock levels in December and holiday periods by 20–30% above baseline based on historical seasonal uplift patterns observed in EDA.
  3. Run promotions on Wednesdays/Thursdays (lowest natural demand days) to shift weekend traffic and reduce stock-out risk during peak days.
  4. Set up automated reorder triggers based on rolling_mean_7 dropping more than 15% below rolling_mean_30 — this signals a demand slowdown.
  5. Expand the model to store-product level — the current aggregate model can be replicated for each of the 15 store-product combinations to enable SKU-level inventory optimization.
  6. Incorporate external signals (weather, local events, competitor promos) in the next model iteration to improve MAPE below 5%.
